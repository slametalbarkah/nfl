A. J. Brown

Arthur Juan Brown (born June 30, 1997) is an American professional football wide receiver for the Philadelphia Eagles of the National Football League (NFL). He played college football for the Ole Miss Rebels and was selected by the Tennessee Titans in the second round of the 2019 NFL draft. After three seasons with the Titans, Brown was traded to the Eagles ahead of the 2022 season. He is a three-time Pro Bowl and All-Pro member, and a starter for the Eagles’ championship in Super Bowl LIX during the 2024 season.





== Early life ==

Brown was born Arthur Juan Brown on June 30, 1997. His mother claims the middle name on his birth certificate of "Juan" was spelled incorrectly, and that his middle name is actually Jauan.

He attended Starkville High School in Starkville, Mississippi, where he played football and baseball. As a senior at Starkville High, he recorded 83 receptions for 1,371 yards and 13 touchdowns and led his team to a 6A state championship. Brown was named first-team All-State by USA Today, The Clarion-Ledger's Dandy Dozen, and the Mississippi Association of Coaches.

As a top recruit in both football and baseball, Brown became the second player after Kyler Murray, to play in both the Under Armour All-America Football Game and the Under Armour All-America Baseball Game.
